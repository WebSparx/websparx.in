Frequently Asked Questions

What is WebSparx?

WebSparx is an initiative by a team to help others. We at WebSparx develop websites for small businesses, organizations and startups which are in an initial phase and want to go Global.


Who are we guys?

We are just a group of students, who are really interested in gaining experience.


Is the website actually free?

Well yes, you'll be given a "yourwebsite.tk" domain, which will map you on the internet. But if you want to have a ".Com" or any other paid domain, then that will not be free. The development and designing part is totally free.


Okay lets say, I want WebSparx to develop my website, What do i have to do?

Just email us at webmaster@WebSparx.in and we'll get back to you. Alternatively you can also send us a message on our Facebook Page.


What about the web-hosting etc?

We'll take care of that, for you. You don't have to worry about that. And yes its free.


What are the limits for Free hosting?

300 MB, is what our hosting provider provides for free accounts, but usually a site is no more than 20 MBs, if it doesn't contains any multimedia content, like videos.


Will you setup the Email accounts with our domain names, like "yourname@yourwebsite.com"?

Yes, if your domain is a paid domain and you want email accounts, then we'll surely do that. But doing so will involve a third party service for providing free email hosting for your domain.(Limited to 10 accounts per domain)


Everything is free, is the quality of service compromised in any way?

No, the quality and uptime of the website is not compromised in any way. Your website will be up and running 24x7. The service we use at our back end can handle upto 5000 users per minute.


Have you developed any websites?

Yes, head over to the projects section, there you'll find websites developed by us.

Atlast what do you gain from this?

Only experience, and maybe a few well wishers.


Note: We believe in open source development, that is the source code of your website will be visible to everyone, on GitHub, If you don't want us to release the source code, please explicitly mention it.
